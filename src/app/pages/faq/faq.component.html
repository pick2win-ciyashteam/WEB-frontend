/ 01Is my first UCT really free?
Yes - every new verified account gets one free UCT generation. No coin needed, no card needed. After that, you'll need to purchase a coin pack to continue. Email and mobile must both be verified with a 6-digit OTP before the free match can be claimed.
/ 02Why do you ask for both my mobile number and my email?
PICK2WIN uses dual verification - 6-digit OTPs are sent to both your mobile and your email. One mobile = one email = one account. Dual verification prevents multi-account abuse, helps recover access if you lose one channel, and lets us deliver important notifications (lineup updates, payment receipts, account security alerts) reliably. Disposable email and VoIP numbers are blocked.
/ 03Can I create multiple accounts to claim the free match again?
No. PICK2WIN enforces one mobile = one email = one account with device fingerprint deduplication, VoIP blocking, and disposable-email blocking. Multi-account abuse - including using fake mobile numbers or temporary emails to claim repeated free matches - results in permanent suspension of all linked accounts. This rule protects honest users and keeps the free-match offer sustainable.

/ 06How do coin packs work - will I be charged again automatically?
Coin packs are one-time purchases - no subscriptions, no auto-renewal. You buy a pack, coins credit immediately, you spend them when generating UCTs. When you run out, you choose whether to buy another pack. Nothing is ever charged automatically. Stripe processes each transaction individually with 3D Secure verification.
/ 07How long are my coins valid for?
Your coins are valid for 365 days, counted from your most recent purchase.

Active Pack Merge. Whenever you buy a new pack while you still have active coins, your remaining coins merge into the new pack and the whole combined balance is revalidated for a fresh 365 days from that purchase date. Example: buy a 50-coin pack today (valid for 365 days). 100 days later you still have coins left and buy a 100-coin pack - your leftover coins merge with the new 100, and the entire balance is now valid for 365 days from that second purchase.

Expired coins. Coins that already passed their 365-day window before the new purchase stay permanently forfeited - only active (non-expired) coins are merged and revalidated. You can always see your combined balance and its expiry in My Space → Active Coin Lots.
/ 08Can I buy more than one coin pack and combine them?
Yes - you can buy any of the four packs (Starter 10 / Basic 25 / Standard 50 / Pro 100) as many times as you like, and your coins always live as a single combined balance. Buying a new pack while you still have active coins triggers the Active Pack Merge: your remaining coins merge into the new pack and the whole balance is revalidated for a fresh 365 days from that purchase date. So topping up doesn't just add coins - it also extends the validity of the coins you already had. Track your combined balance and its expiry in My Space → Active Coin Lots.

/ 12When can I actually run UCT for a match?
UCT activates only after official lineups are released by the clubs - typically around 60 minutes before kickoff for most major leagues. Before lineup release, the match appears in Lineouts with status WAITING FOR LINEUP - UCT Locked. Once the official starting XI and substitutes are published, the Run UCT button becomes active and the 4-step workflow opens. UCT closes at kickoff.
/ 13Can I run UCT more than once for the same match?
No. Each match supports exactly one successful UCT generation per user. Once UCT generates your 20 teams:
• the associated coin is permanently consumed
• regeneration for that match is blocked
• Run UCT becomes disabled
• you're redirected to My Teams

This rule keeps the platform fair and the coin economy honest. Plan your captaincy and mandate selections before clicking Generate.
/ 14How does substitute player selection work?
Substitute selection is optional and user-controlled. In UI Screen 1, you can view the Playing XI and the substitute players, and optionally select 0-3 substitutes to include in the generation pool.

Substitute selection rules:
• Mandate YES (M-YES) - selected substitute appears in all generated teams
• NA (No Mandate) - no forced inclusion

Substitutes cannot be marked Mandate NO (M-NO). If you don't want a substitute considered, simply don't select them - leave them in the bench pool.
/ 15What's the difference between "Mandate YES" and "Mandate NO"?
Both are optional, user-controlled overrides on UI Screen 2:

• M-YES (select 1-2 players) - these players appear in every generated team. Use for nailed-on starters you absolutely want exposure to.
• M-NO (select 1-2 players) - these players won't appear in any of the 20 teams. M-NO players also cannot appear in Captain or Vice-Captain roles. Use for players you want to fully avoid.
• No Mandate (NA) - neither include nor exclude; the player's participation depends on combination logic and category balancing.

You can use M-YES only, M-NO only, M-YES + M-NO together, or NA only. You cannot mix NA with M-YES or M-NO.

Goalkeeper note: M-YES and M-NO apply only to outfield positions (DEF, MID, FWD). Goalkeepers are handled separately through a dedicated allocation choice - see the next question for details.

/ 16Can I use Mandate YES or Mandate NO on goalkeepers?

No - goalkeepers are intentionally excluded from M-YES and M-NO mandates. This is a deliberate UCT design choice, not a technical limitation. M-YES and M-NO apply only to outfield positions: DEF (Defenders), MID (Midfielders), and FWD (Forwards).

Why? A typical lineup has 6-10 outfielders per position, so mandate logic gives you meaningful coverage choices. Goalkeepers are different: only 2 goalkeepers are available per match and each generated team uses exactly 1. With those numbers, the real question isn't "include or exclude" - it's exposure distribution.

Instead of mandates, goalkeepers get their own allocation choice. You can choose how the 2 goalkeepers are distributed across the 20 generated teams:

  • Goalkeeper A in all teams
  • Goalkeeper B in all teams
  • Balanced distribution across the generated teams

This keeps the goalkeeper decision in your hands while giving you the strategically meaningful choice for that position. For the full design rationale, see Step 04 (Goalkeeper Strategy) in the UCT Guide tab.

/ 17Is choosing the Captain & Vice-Captain mandatory?
Yes. Captain & Vice-Captain configuration is mandatory - without a valid selection, the workflow cannot proceed. You choose between two modes:

• CVC mode - select 2-4 players who rotate as Captain and Vice-Captain across the 20 teams
• C & VC mode - explicitly choose Captains (1-4) and Vice-Captains (2-5) from a fixed matrix
/ 18What's the difference between CVC mode and C & VC mode?
Both control how captaincy is distributed across the 20 generated teams:

• CVC mode - pick 2-4 players. They rotate freely as both Captain and Vice-Captain across the 20 teams. Simpler, broader rotation.
• C & VC mode - explicitly separate Captain candidates from Vice-Captain candidates using a fixed matrix:
  - 1 C x 2-5 VC
  - 2 C x 2-4 VC
  - 3 C x 2-3 VC
  - 4 C x 2 VC

Use CVC when you want broad captaincy coverage, C & VC when you want tight control.
/ 19What happens if 3 or 4 matches release lineups at the same time?
PICK2WIN handles concurrent lineup releases naturally. Each match is independent - you can start UCT for one match, finish it, then start another. The Lineouts tab shows all matches with live lineup-release status. The most time-sensitive matches will rise to the top with Run UCT active. Generation itself completes in seconds, so even three back-to-back matches can be fully configured well before kickoff.

/ 22Do I still need to enter the teams into my fantasy app myself?
Yes. PICK2WIN is a configuration workflow tool - it does not connect to or auto-submit on third-party fantasy teams. After generation, you download a CSV with all 20 teams, then submit them to your chosen fantasy app yourself. The CSV is the durable artifact: keep it safe, you'll need it for entry.
/ 23What happens to my teams after the match kicks off?
Your 20 teams stay fully viewable in My Teams - with team preview and CSV download - right up until the match kicks off. At kickoff the 20 teams are cleared to save storage: only a lightweight match-details record (teams, league, date) remains in My Teams, and the preview and CSV download are no longer available. This keeps the platform lean rather than holding millions of teams forever. Because of this, your downloaded CSV becomes your only lasting copy - download it before kickoff.
/ 24How long does PICK2WIN store my generated teams?
Your 20 teams are stored and fully accessible in My Teams from the moment they are generated until the match's kickoff. At kickoff the team data is dropped (only the match-details record is kept), so generated teams are not retained beyond kickoff. The CSV download is the durable copy - download it as soon as generation completes and keep it safe.
