/ 01Why do you ask for both my mobile number and my email?
PICK2WIN uses dual verification - 6-digit OTPs are sent to both your mobile and your email. One mobile = one email = one account. Dual verification prevents multi-account abuse, helps recover access if you lose one channel, and lets us deliver important notifications (lineup updates, payment receipts, account security alerts) reliably. Disposable email and VoIP numbers are blocked.
/ 02Can I create multiple accounts?
No. PICK2WIN enforces one mobile = one email = one account with device fingerprint deduplication, VoIP blocking, and disposable-email blocking. Multi-account abuse - including using fake mobile numbers or temporary emails to evade this rule - results in permanent suspension of all linked accounts. This protects honest users and keeps the platform fair.
/ 03Is there a minimum age to use PICK2WIN?
Yes. PICK2WIN services are strictly intended only for users aged 18 years or older. By creating an account, users confirm they are 18+ and legally permitted to use the platform within their jurisdiction. PICK2WIN is not intended for minors.
/ 04If I delete my account, will I get my unused coins back?
No. If you voluntarily delete your PICK2WIN account, unused coins are permanently forfeited and balances are non-refundable. This is explicit in the Refund Policy and Terms. Before requesting account deletion, fully utilise your purchased coins and complete intended UCT usage. Once deletion is processed, remaining balances are permanently removed and recovery is not possible.
/ 05How do coin packs work - will I be charged again automatically?
Coin packs are one-time purchases - no subscriptions, no auto-renewal. You buy a pack, coins credit immediately, you spend them when generating UCTs. When you run out, you choose whether to buy another pack. Nothing is ever charged automatically. Razorpay processes each transaction individually with 3D Secure verification.
/ 06How long are my coins valid for?
Your coins are valid for 365 days, counted from your most recent purchase.

Active Pack Merge. Whenever you buy a new pack while you still have active coins, your remaining coins merge into the new pack and the whole combined balance is revalidated for a fresh 365 days from that purchase date. Example: buy a 50-coin pack today (valid for 365 days). 100 days later you still have coins left and buy a 100-coin pack - your leftover coins merge with the new 100, and the entire balance is now valid for 365 days from that second purchase.

Expired coins. Coins that already passed their 365-day window before the new purchase stay permanently forfeited - only active (non-expired) coins are merged and revalidated. You can always see your combined balance and its expiry in My Space → Active Coin Lots.
/ 07Can I buy more than one coin pack and combine them?
Yes - you can buy any of the four packs (Starter 5 / Basic 10 / Standard 25 / Pro 50) as many times as you like, and your coins always live as a single combined balance. Buying a new pack while you still have active coins triggers the Active Pack Merge: your remaining coins merge into the new pack and the whole balance is revalidated for a fresh 365 days from that purchase date. So topping up doesn't just add coins - it also extends the validity of the coins you already had. Track your combined balance and its expiry in My Space → Active Coin Lots.

/ 11When can I actually run UCT for a match?
UCT activates only after official lineups are released by the clubs - typically around 60 minutes before kickoff for most major leagues. Before lineup release, the match appears in Lineouts with status WAITING FOR LINEUP - UCT Locked. Once the official starting XI and substitutes are published, the Run UCT button becomes active and the 4-step workflow opens. UCT closes at kickoff.
/ 12Can I run UCT more than once for the same match?
No. Each match supports exactly one successful UCT generation per user. Once UCT generates your 20 teams:
• the associated coin is permanently consumed
• regeneration for that match is blocked
• Run UCT becomes disabled
• you're redirected to My Teams

This rule keeps the platform fair and the coin economy honest. Plan your captaincy and mandate selections before clicking Generate.
/ 13How does substitute player selection work?
Substitute selection is optional and user-controlled. In UI Screen 1, you can view the Playing XI and the substitute players, and optionally select 0-3 substitutes to include in the generation pool.

Substitute selection rules:
• Mandate YES (M-YES) - selected substitute appears in all generated teams
• NA (No Mandate) - no forced inclusion

Substitutes cannot be marked Mandate NO (M-NO). If you don't want a substitute considered, simply don't select them - leave them in the bench pool.
/ 14What's the difference between "Mandate YES" and "Mandate NO"?
Both are optional, user-controlled overrides on UI Screen 2:

• M-YES (select 1-2 players) - these players appear in every generated team. Use for nailed-on starters you absolutely want exposure to.
• M-NO (select 1-2 players) - these players won't appear in any of the 20 teams. M-NO players also cannot appear in Captain or Vice-Captain roles. Use for players you want to fully avoid.
• No Mandate (NA) - neither include nor exclude; the player's participation depends on combination logic and category balancing.

You can use M-YES only, M-NO only, M-YES + M-NO together, or NA only. You cannot mix NA with M-YES or M-NO.

Goalkeeper note: If My Squad has one Goalkeeper, that Goalkeeper is automatically included in all generated teams and cannot be selected as M-YES. If My Squad has two Goalkeepers, you may optionally select one Goalkeeper as M-YES. Two Goalkeepers as M-YES is not allowed.

/ 15Can I use Mandate YES or Mandate NO on goalkeepers?

Yes, but only when My Squad contains two Goalkeepers. If My Squad contains one Goalkeeper, that Goalkeeper is automatically included in all 20 generated teams because every Sorare team requires exactly one Goalkeeper. Selecting that player as M-YES is rejected because it has no effect.

Goalkeeper mandate rules:

  • 1 Goalkeeper in My Squad: automatically included in all 20 teams; M-YES is not allowed.
  • 2 Goalkeepers in My Squad: you may leave both unselected, or select one Goalkeeper as M-YES.
  • 2 Goalkeepers selected as M-YES: not allowed.

This keeps the one-GK case automatic and lets users decide only when there are two Goalkeepers available.

/ 16Is choosing the Captain & Vice-Captain mandatory?
Yes. Captain & Vice-Captain configuration is mandatory - without a valid selection, the workflow cannot proceed. You choose between two modes:

• CVC mode - select 2-4 players who rotate as Captain and Vice-Captain across the 20 teams
• C & VC mode - explicitly choose Captains (1-4) and Vice-Captains (2-5) from a fixed matrix
/ 17What's the difference between CVC mode and C & VC mode?
Both control how captaincy is distributed across the 20 generated teams:

• CVC mode - pick 2-4 players. They rotate freely as both Captain and Vice-Captain across the 20 teams. Simpler, broader rotation.
• C & VC mode - explicitly separate Captain candidates from Vice-Captain candidates using a fixed matrix:
  - 1 C x 2-5 VC
  - 2 C x 2-4 VC
  - 3 C x 2-3 VC
  - 4 C x 2 VC

Use CVC when you want broad captaincy coverage, C & VC when you want tight control.
/ 18What happens if 3 or 4 matches release lineups at the same time?
PICK2WIN handles concurrent lineup releases naturally. Each match is independent - you can start UCT for one match, finish it, then start another. The Lineouts tab shows all matches with live lineup-release status. The most time-sensitive matches will rise to the top with Run UCT active. Generation itself completes in seconds, so even three back-to-back matches can be fully configured well before kickoff.

/ 21Does PICK2WIN submit or enter the teams for me?
No. PICK2WIN is a configuration workflow tool - it does not connect to, integrate with, or auto-submit anywhere outside itself. After generation, you download a TXT with all 20 teams; from there, what you do with them is entirely your own choice. The TXT is the durable artifact - keep it safe.
/ 22What happens to my teams after the match kicks off?
Your 20 teams stay fully viewable in My Teams - with team preview and TXT download - right up until the match kicks off. At kickoff the 20 teams are cleared to save storage: only a lightweight match-details record (teams, league, date) remains in My Teams, and the preview and TXT download are no longer available. This keeps the platform lean rather than holding millions of teams forever. Because of this, your downloaded TXT becomes your only lasting copy - download it before kickoff.
/ 23How long does PICK2WIN store my generated teams?
Your 20 teams are stored and fully accessible in My Teams from the moment they are generated until the match's kickoff. At kickoff the team data is dropped (only the match-details record is kept), so generated teams are not retained beyond kickoff. The TXT download is the durable copy - download it as soon as generation completes and keep it safe.

Football data is sourced from a third-party football API provider - covering exactly the leagues PICK2WIN operates across. / 28What happens if a lineup is updated after I've already generated my teams?
PICK2WIN relies on third-party football data providers, and in rare cases a lineup may be updated after users have already generated their UCT teams - a player removed from or added to the Playing XI, or a lineup correction from the data provider.

Our approach: users who haven't yet generated will automatically see the latest available lineup. Users who have already generated can still follow the lineup info shown on their target platform.

Important: UCT generation completed using the lineup information available at the time of generation. Lineup updates received later do NOT automatically trigger UCT regeneration, additional UCT runs, or additional coin deductions. Read the full Transparency Notice ->

Email support@pick2win.io straight away and attach your downloaded TXT file - it carries the match and configuration details, so we can investigate quickly. (This is different from a lineup-data mismatch above: it's specifically about UCT honouring your inputs.)
