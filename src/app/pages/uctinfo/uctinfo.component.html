User Configuration Teams UCT.

THE ENGINE BEHIND PICK2WIN

A structured workflow tool - not a fantasy app.

UCT (User Configuration Teams) is a structured combination engine. It activates strictly after official lineups confirm, takes your 4-step configuration, and produces 20 structured football virtual teams in a few seconds. UCT is not a prediction engine, not an AI model, and not a betting bot.

01 - WHAT IS UCT

A workflow tool that compresses ~60 minutes of manual team-building into seconds.

UCT stands for User Configuration Teams. It is designed for fantasy football users who create strictly 20 teams per match, manually rotate captains, vary formations, balance player exposure, and race the 60-75 minute pre-kickoff window.

UCT is not a fantasy app replacement. It does not host contests, track points, or run scoring systems. It produces a CSV-exportable batch of 20 structured teams from your configuration, which you then submit to your fantasy contest app of choice.

01b - TEAM COMPOSITION SPEC

Each structured team is an 11-player squad drawn from both real teams.

Every one of the 20 teams generated by UCT follows football-real composition rules. The engine never invents players and never recommends picks. It uses the official playing XI plus substitute pool, applies category constraints, and generates structurally diverse teams from your selection pool.

02 - HOW UCT WORKS - 4-SCREEN WORKFLOW

Four configuration screens, one confirmation, 20 teams.

The workflow runs after official lineups release. The user moves through Substitute, Mandate, Captaincy, and Configuration Summary screens, confirms responsibility, and the engine generates 20 teams strictly from the user's inputs.

1. Substitute ↓ 2. Mandate ↓ 3. CVC / C&VC ↓ 4. Summary ↓ Create / Submit ↓ Confirmation ↓ 20 Teams Generated
